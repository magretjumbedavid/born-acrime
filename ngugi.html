Ngũgĩ wa Thiong'o

Ngũgĩ wa Thiong'o

Ngũgĩ wa Thiong'o is a Kenyan author and academic, who has been described as "East Africa's leading novelist". Ngũgĩ wa Thiong'o's achievements are numerous and diverse, spanning literary innovation, intellectual contributions, and social activism. He is recognized as a major postcolonial theorist and a leading voice in African literature, celebrated for his novels, essays, and plays. He has also been a vocal advocate for the use of African languages and the decolonization of the mind. His essay collection, Decolonising the Mind: The Politics of Language in African Literature, is a seminal work that examines the role of language and culture in the process of colonialism and liberation. He also wrote plays, including The Trial of Dedan Kimathi, which challenged the political and cultural norms of the time.

Biography

Ngũgĩ was born in Kamiriithu, near Limuru in Kiambu district, Kenya, of Kikuyu descent, and baptised James Ngugi. He began writing in English, switching to write primarily in Gikuyu. His work includes novels, plays, short stories, and essays, ranging from literary and social criticism to children's literature. He is the founder and editor of the Gikuyu-language journal Mũtĩiri.
